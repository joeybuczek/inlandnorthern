Safety and Compliance


At the Inland Northern Railroad, safety is our top priority. As a leading transportation provider handling agricultural and volatile chemical freight loads across the midwest, we are committed to upholding the highest standards of safety and compliance. We recognize the importance of integrating cutting-edge technologies and stringent safety regulations into our operations to ensure the well-being of our employees, customers, and communities.

In accordance with the Federal Railroad Administration (FRA) regulations, our dedicated team undergoes rigorous training and certification programs to maintain compliance with safety protocols. From conducting thorough inspections of our railcars and locomotives to implementing state-of-the-art safety equipment, we leave no stone unturned in safeguarding the integrity of our transportation infrastructure.

In addition to adhering to federal mandates, we work closely with local authorities and regulatory bodies to address regional safety concerns and mitigate potential risks associated with transporting agricultural and volatile chemical freight. Our proactive approach to safety includes regular maintenance of track infrastructure, monitoring weather conditions, and implementing emergency response protocols to swiftly address any unforeseen incidents.

Furthermore, we understand the importance of environmental stewardship in our operations. With the advent of environmentally friendly practices gaining momentum, we actively seek innovative solutions to minimize our carbon footprint and reduce emissions. Through strategic partnerships and investment in eco-friendly technologies, we strive to contribute to a sustainable future while ensuring the safe transportation of essential goods.

The Inland Northern remains steadfast in our commitment to safety and compliance, and we continually seek opportunities to enhance our safety protocols and exceed regulatory expectations. Together, we can build a safer and more resilient transportation network across the heartland for generations to come.
